## Decisions Made
- **Teaching sequence:** LLM fundamentals → chat limitations → why agents matter → prompt principles → context engineering → agents overview → skills design
- **Session structure:** 6 sessions × 1.5–2 hours each = 10 hours total
- **Balance:** 40% conceptual, 50% hands-on practice, 10% governance/risk
- **Audience calibration:** Business users (non-technical); avoid heavy math; use Vietnamese business examples (marketing, teaching, manufacturing)

## Key Findings
1. **MCP adoption velocity is real:** 100K downloads (Nov 2024) → 8M (Apr 2025). Becoming industry standard across OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, AWS
2. **Agent vs. chat ROI is quantifiable:** Agents reduce task time by 60–85%; reduce errors 70–95%. Concrete messaging needed for business buy-in
3. **Context engineering is emerging discipline:** Smart zone (40–60% utilization) + RPI workflow + FIC = critical framework for 10+ hour projects. Not yet widely known by business users
4. **Skills/custom workflows are accessible:** Modern platforms use drag-and-drop + natural language; no coding required. 92% of execs expect AI workflows by 2025
5. **Security/governance gap:** MCP has known risks (prompt injection, tool permission overreach). Course should include mitigation patterns
