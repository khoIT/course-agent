# Course Design Quality Evaluation Criteria (Part 1: Criteria 1-10)

> Rating scale: P (Pass) | NI (Needs Improvement) | NA (Not Addressed)

## I. Target Learners (Criteria 1-2)

### Criterion 1: Clear Identification of Target Learners
- Explicitly states who the primary learners are
- Identifies primary vs secondary learner groups
- **Pass**: Target learner group clearly defined with specifics (role, level, context)
- **NI**: Learners mentioned but vaguely described
- **NA**: No learner identification

### Criterion 2: Detailed Learner Profile (KSA)
- Describes existing Knowledge, Skills, and Attitudes
- Considers learning styles (Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic)
- Accounts for learning conditions (time, environment)
- **Pass**: Comprehensive portrait covering KSA + learning preferences + conditions
- **NI**: Partial description, missing one or more KSA dimensions
- **NA**: No learner profile provided

## II. Course Objectives (Criteria 3-5)

### Criterion 3: Learner-Centered Objectives
- Written from learner's perspective (not trainer's)
- States what learners will be able to DO after the course
- **Pass**: All objectives clearly learner-centered using action verbs
- **NI**: Mix of learner-centered and trainer-centered objectives
- **NA**: Objectives written from trainer's perspective or missing

### Criterion 4: Appropriate Objective Type and Level
- Types (Knowledge/Skill/Attitude) match learner needs
- Cognitive levels (Bloom's) appropriate for audience
- Three levels: Understand & Retain, Apply, Problem-Solve
- **Pass**: Types and levels clearly aligned with learner needs
- **NI**: Some mismatch between levels and needs
- **NA**: No consideration of types/levels

### Criterion 5: Clear and Measurable Objectives
- Uses observable, measurable action verbs (avoid "understand", "know")
- Formula: Action Verb + Standard/Criteria + Condition
- Meets SMART criteria
- **Pass**: All objectives use measurable verbs with clear standards and conditions
- **NI**: Some objectives lack measurability or specificity
- **NA**: Objectives vague or unmeasurable

## III. Content Structure (Criteria 6-7)

### Criterion 6: Focus on Essential Content
- Categorized: Must Have / Should Have / Nice to Have
- Eliminates content overload by focusing on learner NEEDS
- **Pass**: Content clearly prioritized; focused on essential items
- **NI**: Some unnecessary content; prioritization unclear
- **NA**: Content is a "brain dump" without prioritization

### Criterion 7: Logical Content Sequencing (WHY-WHAT-HOW-SO WHAT)
- WHY → WHAT → HOW → SO WHAT flow throughout
- Sequencing: simple→complex, known→unknown, theory→practice
- **Pass**: Clear WHY-WHAT-HOW-SO WHAT structure throughout
- **NI**: Partial logical flow; some sections out of sequence
- **NA**: No discernible sequencing logic

## IV. Lesson Plan (Criteria 8-10)

### Criterion 8: Clear Content Delivery Approach
- Specifies inductive and/or deductive approach per section
- Steps for delivering each content point clearly outlined
- **Pass**: Each section has clear delivery approach with step-by-step flow
- **NI**: Some sections lack delivery specifications
- **NA**: No delivery approach described

### Criterion 9: Supporting Resources Identified
- Specifies materials needed (handouts, references, tools)
- Resources aligned with content and activities
- **Pass**: All resources clearly listed and aligned
- **NI**: Resources partially listed or misaligned
- **NA**: No supporting resources mentioned

### Criterion 10: Reasonable Time Allocation
- Estimated time per section specified
- Realistic and proportional to content importance
- Balances presentation, practice, and discussion
- **Pass**: Time estimates for all sections; realistic and well-balanced
- **NI**: Incomplete or unrealistic estimates
- **NA**: No time allocation provided
