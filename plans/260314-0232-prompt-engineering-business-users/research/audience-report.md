# Audience Analysis Report
## Prompt Engineering for Business Users (Vietnam)

**Researcher:** Audience Analyst
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Confidence Level:** Medium (Global data high; Vietnam-specific limited)

---

## Executive Summary

Target learners (managers, marketers, teachers in Vietnam) face 4 critical barriers: **low prompt engineering literacy**, **limited CLI comfort**, **accuracy/trust concerns**, and **role-specific pressure to adopt fast**. Vietnamese workplace culture favors hierarchy + rote learning, but digital adoption is accelerating (11% tech-ready → 28% CAGR growth). Language preference is Vietnamese-dominant; English tech terms mixed into Vietnamese text is "an immediate turn-off."

---

## 1. Current AI Tool Adoption (Vietnam + Global)

### Global Market Reality
- **ChatGPT dominance:** 68% market share (down from 87% one year prior); 2.8B MAU
- **Claude growth:** Fastest growth rate late 2025; 70% Fortune 100 adoption; 46% for work + 19% study
- **Google Gemini:** 18.2% share (explosive 5.4% → 18.2% in 12 months)
- **Professional preference:** 70.8% choose ChatGPT; 31% Gemini; 32% Google Search AI

### Vietnam-Specific Gaps
- **No published Vietnam-specific chatbot adoption data** (not available in 2026 sources)
- **Assumed:** ChatGPT dominates due to market leadership; Claude underutilized due to awareness/ease-of-use
- **Implication:** Course must work with both platforms; include ChatGPT-first examples with Claude as advanced alternative

### Vietnam's Digital Economy Context
- Growing 20%+ annually; expected to reach $49B by 2025 (from $23B in 2022)
- Government mandate: All ministry + provincial leaders must conduct operations digitally by June 2025
- **Critical gap:** Only 11% of workforce has Industry 4.0 digital skills; 1.5M specialists training planned by 2030

---

## 2. Target Learner Pain Points & Barriers

### Barrier 1: Skill Gaps & Confidence Deficit
- **51% of business leaders** lack sufficient AI knowledge at management level
- **40% of enterprises** report inadequate internal AI expertise
- **Fear of replacement** + entrenched workflows derail adoption even with tools in hand
- **First impression matters:** Frustrating early experiences stick; learners abandon tools after bad initial attempts

### Barrier 2: Accuracy + Trust Concerns
- **~50% of organizations** worry AI accuracy/bias is a barrier to adoption
- **SMEs especially skeptical:** Demand robust oversight mechanisms
- **Implication:** Teach error detection + validation techniques; build trust through guardrails, not hype

### Barrier 3: CLI/Technical Barriers (Direct to Target)
- **Non-technical users cannot use command-line tools** (Python, LangChain, etc.)
- **Lack of intuitive interfaces** overwhelming for business users
- **Problem:** Prompt iteration locked behind developer handoffs (not self-service)
- **Solution space:** No-code platforms (PromptLayer, Lilypad) emerging; glossy GUI > CLI for non-devs

### Barrier 4: Poor ROI & Practical Application
- **95% of AI pilots failing** (MIT report); only ~5% achieve rapid revenue lift
- **<20% scaled across enterprise** after initial success
- **Pain:** Leaders see experiments, not bottom-line impact; skepticism spreads
- **Implication:** Course must link every prompt technique to **concrete business outcomes** (productivity gains, cost reduction)

---

## 3. Vietnamese Business Culture & Learning Preferences

### Hierarchical, Teacher-Centered Norms
- **Confucianism shapes workplace:** Respect for hierarchy, authority deference, collectivism over individualism
- **Traditional pedagogy:** Rote memory, grammar-translation method, teacher-centered instruction
- **Risk:** Passive listening dominant; active problem-solving undervalued
- **Opportunity:** Respect instructor authority; frame prompt engineering as "skill mastery via discipline"

### Critical Thinking Gap
- Cultural norms emphasize hierarchy + obedience over critical thinking
- **Only 14%** of Vietnamese workforce has "clear view of skills to acquire" (PwC 2021)
- **Implication:** Explicitly teach **"why" + "when" prompt techniques work**; don't assume learners will extrapolate

### Digital Readiness (Mixed Signals)
- **Youthful population, mobile-first culture, rapidly expanding economy** = digital appetite
- **BUT:** Limited digital literacy among workforce; skill gaps real
- **Digital adoption drivers:** Top management support, competitive pressure, financial resources, tech development
- **Implication:** Appeal to competitive advantage (keeping up with peers) + manager mandate

---

## 4. Language Preferences & Localization

### Vietnamese > English
- **Government mandate:** English as second language 2025–2035; proficiency rising
- **BUT:** "An English word anywhere in a sentence is an immediate turn-off to Vietnamese readers"
- **Tech vocabulary emerging:** "lập trình viên" (software developer) normalized; mixed English/Vietnamese jarring
- **Recommendation:** Teach in Vietnamese; translate all technical terms into Vietnamese equivalents; avoid English-Vietnamese code-switching

### Northern (Hanoi) Dialect Preference
- "The official preference and more relevant for business"
- **Implication:** If hiring Vietnamese instructors, prefer Hanoi-based or standardized Northern dialect

---

## 5. Motivations for Prompt Engineering Adoption

### Push Factors (Competitive Pressure)
- Peers using AI; risk of falling behind
- Manager mandate: "Adopt AI or lose efficiency edge"
- Job security: Reskilling narrative (automation = learn or be replaced)

### Pull Factors (Personal)
- Productivity gains (save time, reduce tedium)
- Curiosity about new tool capability
- **Teachers especially:** Student engagement via AI examples; lesson prep acceleration

---

## Key Design Implications

| Learner Profile | Pain Point | Course Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Managers (Time-poor, ROI-focused)** | Busy; want quick wins; skeptical ROI | Start with 15-min case studies showing $impact; Fast-win activities (first week) |
| **Marketers (Copy-focused, iterative)** | Don't know how to "ask" AI for good copy; accuracy concerns | Teach copy-specific prompts; error detection; A/B testing workflow |
| **Teachers (Pedagogical concerns, low tech comfort)** | Worry about plagiarism, student creativity loss; intimidated by CLI | Focus on interactive/exam design prompts; no CLI required; ethical framing |
| **All (Vietnamese culture)** | Prefer hierarchy + authority; rote learning safe | Instructor credibility paramount; structured drill-based activities; clear "rules" |
| **All (English avoidance)** | English terms confusing, demotivating | Full Vietnamese translation; minimal code/CLI; glossary of Vietnamese tech terms |

## Unresolved Questions

1. **Exact Vietnam ChatGPT/Claude market split:** No published data found. Recommend user survey early cohort.
2. **Teacher-specific pain points:** Limited research on Vietnamese educator adoption of AI. Recommend SME interview.
3. **Cost sensitivity:** No data on Vietnamese learner willingness to pay for training. Affects delivery model (free/freemium vs. paid).

---

## Recommended Next Steps for Course Design

1. **Validate language preference:** Test Vietnamese vs. mixed-language materials with 3-5 learners before final production
2. **Build trust early:** Lead with "AI accuracy limits + how to catch errors" before deep prompt techniques
3. **Fast-win first week:** Deliver 1 concrete business outcome in Session 1 (manager sales email, teacher exam rubric, marketer headline) to prove ROI
4. **No CLI.** Use ChatGPT web UI + Claude web UI exclusively. No Python, no command line.
5. **Hierarchical pedagogy:** Instructor is authority figure. Structured activities > open-ended discussion.
