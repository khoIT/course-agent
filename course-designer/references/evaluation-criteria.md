# Course Design Quality Evaluation Criteria

> Source: LearnSmart - "Danh gia chat luong thiet ke khoa hoc" (extended with accessibility criterion)
> Rating scale: P (Pass) | NI (Needs Improvement) | NA (Not Addressed)

## How to Use This Checklist

Evaluate each criterion against the course materials. For each criterion, assign a rating and provide specific comments referencing the actual course content. A well-designed course should achieve "Pass" on all 18 criteria.

---

## I. Target Learners (Criteria 1-2)

### Criterion 1: Clear Identification of Target Learners
- Explicitly states who the primary learners are
- Identifies primary vs secondary learner groups
- **Pass**: Target learner group clearly defined with specifics (role, level, context)
- **Needs Improvement**: Learners mentioned but vaguely described
- **Not Addressed**: No learner identification

### Criterion 2: Detailed Learner Profile (KSA)
- Describes learners' existing Knowledge, Skills, and Attitudes related to the course topic
- Considers learning styles (Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic)
- Accounts for learning conditions (time available, environment)
- **Pass**: Comprehensive learner portrait covering KSA + learning preferences + conditions
- **Needs Improvement**: Partial description, missing one or more KSA dimensions
- **Not Addressed**: No learner profile provided

---

## II. Course Objectives (Criteria 3-5)

### Criterion 3: Learner-Centered Objectives
- Objectives written from the learner's perspective (not the trainer's)
- States what learners will be able to DO after the course
- **Pass**: All objectives clearly learner-centered using action verbs
- **Needs Improvement**: Mix of learner-centered and trainer-centered objectives
- **Not Addressed**: Objectives written from trainer's perspective or missing

### Criterion 4: Appropriate Objective Type and Level
- Objective types (Knowledge/Skill/Attitude) match learner needs
- Cognitive levels (Bloom's taxonomy) appropriate for the audience
- Three levels considered: Understand & Retain, Apply, Problem-Solve
- **Pass**: Objective types and levels clearly aligned with identified learner needs
- **Needs Improvement**: Some mismatch between objective levels and learner needs
- **Not Addressed**: No consideration of objective types/levels

### Criterion 5: Clear and Measurable Objectives
- Uses observable, measurable action verbs (avoid "understand", "know", "appreciate")
- Follows formula: Action Verb + Standard/Criteria + Condition
- Meets SMART criteria (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- **Pass**: All objectives use measurable verbs with clear standards and conditions
- **Needs Improvement**: Some objectives lack measurability or specificity
- **Not Addressed**: Objectives vague or unmeasurable

---

## III. Content Structure / Outline (Criteria 6-7)

### Criterion 6: Focus on Essential Content
- Content categorized by priority: Must Have / Should Have / Nice to Have
- "Must Have" = without this, learners cannot achieve objectives
- "Should Have" = supports easier achievement of objectives
- "Nice to Have" = useful but not critical for objective achievement
- Eliminates content overload by focusing on what learners NEED
- **Pass**: Content clearly prioritized; focused on essential "must have" items
- **Needs Improvement**: Some unnecessary content included; prioritization unclear
- **Not Addressed**: Content appears to be a "brain dump" without prioritization

### Criterion 7: Logical Content Sequencing (WHY-WHAT-HOW-SO WHAT)
- Content follows the logical flow:
  - **WHY**: Why is this content important? What problem does it solve?
  - **WHAT**: Present the content/concepts clearly
  - **HOW**: Practice, application, hands-on activities
  - **SO WHAT**: Real-world application reminders and takeaways
- Sequencing principles: simple→complex, known→unknown, theory→practice
- **Pass**: Content follows clear WHY-WHAT-HOW-SO WHAT structure throughout
- **Needs Improvement**: Partial logical flow; some sections out of sequence
- **Not Addressed**: No discernible content sequencing logic

---

## IV. Lesson Plan (Criteria 8-10)

### Criterion 8: Clear Content Delivery Approach
- Specifies how each content point will be delivered
- Uses inductive and/or deductive approaches:
  - **Inductive (bottom-up)**: Specific examples/experiences → General principles
  - **Deductive (top-down)**: General principles → Specific examples/applications
- Steps for delivering each content point are clearly outlined
- **Pass**: Each content section has clear delivery approach with step-by-step flow
- **Needs Improvement**: Some sections lack delivery specifications
- **Not Addressed**: No delivery approach described

### Criterion 9: Supporting Resources Identified
- Specifies what supporting materials are needed (handouts, references, tools)
- Resources aligned with content and activities
- **Pass**: All supporting resources clearly listed and aligned with content
- **Needs Improvement**: Resources partially listed or misaligned
- **Not Addressed**: No supporting resources mentioned

### Criterion 10: Reasonable Time Allocation
- Estimated time for each content section is specified
- Time allocation is realistic and proportional to content importance
- Balances presentation, practice, and discussion time
- **Pass**: Time estimates provided for all sections; realistic and well-balanced
- **Needs Improvement**: Time estimates incomplete or seem unrealistic
- **Not Addressed**: No time allocation provided

---

## V. Content Slides (Criteria 11-13)

### Criterion 11: Slide Master Created
- Uses a consistent Slide Master template
- Ensures visual consistency across all slides
- **Pass**: Slide Master properly set up with consistent branding/layout
- **Needs Improvement**: Inconsistent slide formatting
- **Not Addressed**: No Slide Master used

### Criterion 12: Design Principles Applied
- Follows four slide design principles:
  1. **Easy to Read** (KISS: Keep It Short and Simple)
  2. **Easy to Understand** (meaningful images replace text)
  3. **Easy to Remember** (highlight key content with large fonts, bold colors)
  4. **Dynamic/Engaging** (warm+cool color contrast, ~30% warm colors, consistent color usage, warm colors for focal points)
- **Pass**: All four design principles clearly demonstrated
- **Needs Improvement**: Some principles applied but not consistently
- **Not Addressed**: Slides are text-heavy with no design consideration

### Criterion 13: Dynamic and Engaging Slides
- Contains illustrative images and visuals
- Uses SmartArt for diagrams and concepts
- Includes presentation effects/animations
- One key message per slide
- Replaces text with visuals where possible
- **Pass**: Slides are visually rich, dynamic, and engaging
- **Needs Improvement**: Some visual elements but mostly text-based
- **Not Addressed**: Plain text slides with no visual enhancement

---

## VI. Learning Activities (Criteria 14-18)

### Criterion 14: Clear Activity Objectives
- Each activity has a clear learning objective
- Activity objectives tie directly to course content
- **Pass**: Every activity has explicit objectives linked to specific content
- **Needs Improvement**: Some activities lack clear objectives or content connection
- **Not Addressed**: Activities have no stated objectives

### Criterion 15: Activities Meet REAL Criteria
- **R**elevant: Activity directly relates to course content and learner context
- **E**ngaging: Activity captures learner interest and participation
- **A**ctive: Learners are doing, not just watching/listening
- **L**earner-centered: Activity builds on learner experience and knowledge
- **Pass**: Activities clearly meet all four REAL criteria
- **Needs Improvement**: Activities meet some but not all REAL criteria
- **Not Addressed**: Activities are passive or irrelevant

### Criterion 16: Detailed Activity Procedures
- Step-by-step instructions for each activity
- Clear facilitator actions and learner actions at each step
- Timing for each step specified
- **Pass**: Activity procedures are detailed enough for any facilitator to execute
- **Needs Improvement**: Procedures are too vague or too brief
- **Not Addressed**: No activity procedures provided

### Criterion 17: Complete Activity Materials
- All materials needed for activities are designed and prepared
- Includes: worksheets, case studies, role-play scenarios, game materials, etc.
- Materials are self-explanatory and ready to use
- **Pass**: All activity materials are complete, well-designed, and ready for use
- **Needs Improvement**: Some materials missing or incomplete
- **Not Addressed**: No activity materials prepared

### Criterion 18: Activity Debrief Connects to Content
- Each activity concludes with a structured debrief/wrap-up
- Debrief explicitly connects activity outcomes back to course content
- Key takeaways from the activity are summarized
- **Pass**: Every activity has a debrief that clearly bridges to course content
- **Needs Improvement**: Debriefs exist but don't clearly connect to content
- **Not Addressed**: No activity debriefs

---

## VII. Accessibility & Inclusion (Criterion 19)

### Criterion 19: Accessible and Inclusive Design
- Slides use sufficient color contrast (WCAG AA: 4.5:1 for text, 3:1 for large text)
- Meaning is never conveyed by color alone (use shapes, labels, or patterns alongside color)
- Activities offer multi-modal alternatives (not all visual, not all verbal, not all physical)
- Language is inclusive and avoids unnecessary jargon or culturally specific idioms without explanation
- Handouts and materials are readable by screen readers (proper heading hierarchy, alt-text guidance for images)
- Considers diverse learner needs: language proficiency, physical abilities, neurodiversity
- **Pass**: Materials demonstrate conscious accessibility choices across slides, activities, and handouts
- **Needs Improvement**: Some accessibility considered but gaps remain (e.g., color-only indicators, single-modality activities)
- **Not Addressed**: No accessibility considerations in any materials
